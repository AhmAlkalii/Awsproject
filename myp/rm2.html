More DC

DC Comics, the company behind the DC Universe, was founded in 1934 by Malcolm Wheeler-Nicholson.
The first DC comic book, "New Fun: The Big Comic Magazine," was published in 1935 and introduced a new concept to the comic book world:
the comic book as a compilation of shorter stories. In 1938, DC Comics published "Action Comics #1," which introduced Superman to the world.
This was a game-changer for the comic book industry and set the stage for the creation of many more iconic characters like Batman,
Wonder Woman, The Flash, Green Lantern, and Aquaman. Over the years, DC Comics has expanded its universe and brought countless stories to life through comic books,
movies, TV shows, and video games. With its rich history and diverse cast of characters,
the DC Universe continues to captivate audiences and inspire new generations of fans.

The Future

James Gunn, best known for his work on Marvel's Guardians of the Galaxy, joined the DC Universe in 2018 to write and direct the Suicide Squad sequel.
Gunn's take on the anti-hero team was praised for its irreverent humor, unique characters, and stylized action.
The film featured an all-star cast including Margot Robbie, Idris Elba, John Cena, and Sylvester Stallone, among others. Following the success of The Suicide Squad, Gunn was tapped to write and direct a spin-off series for the character Peacemaker,
played by John Cena. The series was released in 2022 and continued to showcase Gunn's distinctive style and offbeat humor.
Fans eagerly await to see what other projects Gunn has in store for the DC Universe,
as he continues to bring fresh and exciting ideas to the world of superheroes and villains.
